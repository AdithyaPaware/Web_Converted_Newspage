My brother was killed for overtaking Atiq's brother's car:UP man.

A man from UP has revealed that his younger brother was killed for allegedly overtaking a car that belonged to Atiq Ahmed's brother Ashraf in UP. The man revealed Atiq said, "Ye tum accha nahi kiye" (You made a mistake) when he was called to the slain gangster's 'court'. He said his brother was killed a few days later.

MOscow bans unauthorised drones after Putin 'assassination' bid.

Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin on Wednesday announced a ban on unauthorised drone flights over the city after two Ukrainian drones were alleged to have targeted Russian President Vladimir Putin. The drones, aimed at Kremlin in an alleged assassination bid, were shot down by Russian authorities. Sobyanin said the ban would curb obstruction of the work of law enforcement.

Congress insults Indian Armed Forces: PM Modi in karnataka Rally.

Addressing an election rally in Karnataka's Dakshina Kannada district, PM Narendra Modi said the Congress insults Indian Armed Forces. "The whole country respects and honours our soldiers. We are proud of our soldiers, are we not? The 'reverse gear' Congress party insults our soldiers," said PM Modi. He also accused Congress of protecting "anti-national" people.

Actor-Director Manobala passes away at 69.

Tamil actor-director Manobala has passed away at the age of 69 in Chennai. While the cause of his death is still unclear, Manobala was reportedly hospitalised for the past two weeks. Actor-director GM Kumar shared the news of Manobala's demise on Twitter and said, "Manobala Sir passed away."

Actor Kevin Costner & his wife Christine announce divorce after 19 years.

Actor and filmmaker Kevin Costner and his wife and former model Christine Baumgartner are divorcing after nearly 19 years of marriage, a representative for the actor said on Tuesday. Costner and Baumgartner began dating in 1998 and got married in 2004. They have two sons, aged 14 and 15, and a 12-year-old daughter together.

NYC Mayor to give hundreds of free Air Tags to reduce car thefts.

New York City Mayor Eric Adams announced that hundreds of free Apple AirTags will be handed out in a bid to reduce the number of car thefts. Adams called the AirTag an "amazing piece of ingenuity" and an "excellent tracking device". A local nonprofit has already donated 500 AirTags to the city and there'll be a fundraiser to buy more.

Twitter's verification rule hit Tata's reputation:Ex-India head.

Former Twitter India head Manish Maheshwari has written to Twitter owner Elon Musk listing five-point recommendations for the platform. In his letter, Maheshwari wrote that Twitter's latest verification policy changes hurt the digital reputation of prominent figures like Ratan Tata, Amitabh Bachchan and Priyanka Chopra. He added that damage has been done to the trust celebrities had in Twitter.

Gambir told Kohli 'Abusing my player is like abusing my family'.

During their altercation, LSG mentor Gautam Gambhir told RCB's Virat Kohli abusing his player is like abusing his family, PTI reported quoting an eyewitness. Kohli reportedly responded to Gambhir saying, "Then you take care of your family." As per the eyewitness, Gambhir then said, "So now...I've to learn from you." Both Kohli and Gambhir were fined 100% of match fee.

Spreads venom, demonises full community:Sibal on The Kerala Story.

Rajya Sabha MP Kapil Sibal criticised the Central Board of Film Certification (CBFC) for clearing the movie 'The Kerala Story' and claimed that it "spreads venom and demonises an entire community". The film claims that 32,000 women in Kerala converted to Islam and were recruited by ISIS. Notably, Supreme Court also refused to entertain pleas on staying the film's release.

Russia ordered to immediately stop Ukraine invasion by International Court of justice.

The UN's highest court, the International Court of Justice (ICJ), on Wednesday ordered Russia to immediately stop invasion of Ukraine. "The order is binding under international law. Russia must comply immediately. Ignoring the order will isolate Russia even further," tweeted Ukraine President Volodymyr Zelenskyy. "Ukraine gained a complete victory in its case against Russia at the ICJ," he wrote.
